> And it's a pain and a strain to the eye if you ever have to look at such a

If you bothered to read the text or the Elastic Tabstops URL I posted
you would have had your answer. It summary. If you use a non-ET enabled
editor, simply setting the tab stop gap to 8 spaces gives you near
identical results. That's if you used tabs only for indentation,
alignment of text is another story.

> or
> even that very same one with different settingss.

This is just wrong! ET enabled editors allows the user to adjust the
indentation and alignment gaps at will. Text will always look correct
with any ET enabled editor - even if indentation width settings are
different between users. Read the ET website to fully understand how ET
works.


> And beware of mixing TABs and spaces for indentation!

This is common sense and applies to any editor, not just ET enabled ones.


> to recognize your writings if you ever changed your settings, or have to
> use a different editor.

9/10 times developers will use the same editor for the same project.
over and over. So they shouldn't have a problem with editors, because
they will almost always use the same editor. This point is moot.


> settings in a different one is often strenuous, if these "rubber tabs" are

I don't know what "rubber tabs" are. Please get the terminology correct
and try again.


> TABs with spaces "on the fly" (as you type them) as well - but not all of
> them support this feature also.

The author of ET created a plugin for gEdit, giving gEdit ET support.
That plugin has such a function and it works actually quite well. When
files are saved, all tabs characters are replaced with spaced to try and
match the indentation and alignment. When the file is loaded again, the
reverse is done. All on the fly.
