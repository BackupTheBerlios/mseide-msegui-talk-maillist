> Exactly... What can be easier than this:
>
> var
>  lCustomer: TCustomer
> begin
>  lCustomer := TCustomer.Create;
>  lCustomer.Load('xxx');
>   //....
>  lCustomer.Save;

I agree.

> or....
>
> var
>  lList: TCustomerList;
> begin
>  lList := TCustomerList.Create;
>  lList.Filter.GreaterThan('JoinDate', EncodeDate(2010, 1, 1));
>  lList.Read;
>  // we now have a list of customer objects who joined us since 2010-01-01

Humm... agree in part. If you have a simple list, ok... but if you
have a JOIN, complex filters, etc these classes lose their meaning.
IMHO is better transform a ResultSet (TDataSet) in classes - or list
of classes - with helper functions and parameters, ie, you ask to
these functions what result (classes, lists, etc) do you want.
