On 29 July 2011 23:13, Marcos Douglas wrote:
>
> I agree with KISS principle... but an OPF serves to abstract the
> persistence making the code easier to read and reuse.

Exactly... What can be easier than this:

var
  lCustomer: TCustomer
begin
  lCustomer := TCustomer.Create;
  lCustomer.Load('xxx');
   //....
  lCustomer.Save;
  //...


or....

var
  lList: TCustomerList;
begin
  lList := TCustomerList.Create;
  lList.Filter.GreaterThan('JoinDate', EncodeDate(2010, 1, 1));
  lList.Read;
  // we now have a list of customer objects who joined us since 2010-01-01


All SQL statements, transactions, and ever error recovery (rolled back
transactions) and all taken care of by the OPF framework.
