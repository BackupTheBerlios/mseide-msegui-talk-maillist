> The important point being that ET doesn't insert more TAB characters to

Well, it will have to (and usually does) add one TAB for every level of
indentation, which leads to _very_ deep indentation in other editors or
just text viewers.
And beware of mixing TABs and spaces for indentation! You will not be able
to recognize your writings if you ever changed your settings, or have to
use a different editor.

As every compiler or such seems to bring a couple of IDEs these days, you
would have to switch editors too often if you work on a multi language
project - but if working on the files from another IDE with different
settings in a different one is often strenuous, if these "rubber tabs" are
turned on. I've come to _remove_ _any_ TAB characters from files created
that way and _reformat_ all of them to make them comprehensible again.
The _only_ way such a feature is tolerable is to have the editor replace
TABs with spaces "on the fly" (as you type them) as well - but not all of
them support this feature also.

Thus, if this feature should be considered for implementation, it should
strongly be considered to make it _optional_, and to make it _disabled_.by
default.
