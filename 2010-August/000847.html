> > process has to replace the current one _seamlessly_. That's why I
> > deinitialize most everything except the window just before executing
> > the new process, do a fork there and have the child process keep the
...
> What I do not understand, why does "application.terminated:= true;" not
> work?

It does close and remove the form window(s) from the screen _before_ the
program terminates and can be replaced by another one. That is _not_
seamless, and there is a time when no application display is visible.
Though this is not a security problem here because of very limited input
devices, it gives the impression that the application has terminated and
had to be restarted (which in fact has happened indeed), which should be
avoided because it's somewhat distracting for the users.
Terminating the application, thus removing the window(s) may only happen
_after_ the new process has built and displayed its window(s).
