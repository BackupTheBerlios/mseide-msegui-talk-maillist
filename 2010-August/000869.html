On 27 August 2010 14:28, Christian Nobel wrote:
> Off topic:
> Why is it only possible (in Thunderbird??) to have lines that are only 72
> characters long, before wrapping, when using a newsgroup?
> I haven't seen 72 characters since the old IBM punch card in the sixties and
> seventies.

That's got nothing to do with it being a newsgroup. It's all down to
the email or news client. Thunderbird is well know for mangling
patches that are part of the message content, unlike for example Claws
Mail. Thunderbird forces line wrapping, and it's quite a pain to tell
it otherwise.

Here is instructions on how to disable line wrapping. See the URL and
scroll down to the "Thunderbird" heading (line 427).

http://repo.or.cz/w/git.git?a=blob;f=Documentation/SubmittingPatches;hb=HEAD
