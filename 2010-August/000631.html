Graeme Geldenhuys wrote:

> Hi Martin,
> 
> For some time I couldn't figure out why the MSEide editor feels
> strange to me - something I just couldn't get used to. I finally
> figured out what the major obstacles are for me.
> 
> * word jumping: When you navigate words by pressing Ctrl+LeftArrow,
> MSEide's editor places the cursor on the last character of the
> preceding word. All other editors place the cursor before the first
> letter of the current word (after the space between the two words -
> reading left-to-right).
>   The same happens on Ctrl+RightArrow. All other editors places the
> cursor after the last letter of the following word, MSEide places the
> cursor at
>    the beginning of the next word, after the space between the two
> words.  Could this strange behaviour be a toggle option in the Editor
> Options dialog? Obviously you are used to this, so the default option
> can be the way it works now - for your convenience.
> 
I found an even better solution. ;-)
Ctrl+Left searches next word begin, Ctrl+Right next word end (trunk 3835).

> * selecting words with the mouse. Again all other editors (that I
> could find) allow you to double click a word, to select that word.
> This is very handy if you need to copy & paste a word to a new
> location. As a bonus, triple clicking the word, selects the whole line
> of text.
> 
Trunk 3835 has support for ss_triple, Shift+TrplLeftClick selects the whole
line.
