Our apps have very clear separation between UI, Business Objects and
Data Storage layers. We don't use DB-aware components, but rather
standard components via a Model-GUI-Mediator (MGM) design pattern that I
implemented for tiOPF. This allows us to very easily switch our UI to
any available GUI toolkit (VCL, LCL, fpGUI or even MSEgui). tiOPF
already has MGM support for VCL, LCL and fpGUI. MSEgui support should
not be hard to add at all (if you know MSEgui, it will probably take one
days work). Using tiOPF as our persistence layer, we can switch our
relational databases (and related data access components) between
Interbase, Firebird, MySQL, PosgreSQL, Oracle etc by simply changing one
compiler directive and a recompile. So whatever RDBMS our clients run,
we can simply slot into that setup without hassle.

Anyway, from what you have said, I believe your project is already
developed so it's a bit late to switch now to a different design, but I
thought it worth mentioning to you - for any of your future projects.
