Anyway, the "wording"'s still a bit awkward and counters intuition, when
assigning a "variable" its "current value" does anything of significance.

I realize that in programming with objects, the rules are different from
what they are for simple data types, especially when the working of
operators can be modified.
Using properties is one area where this can be done with FPC / Object
Pascal, even though this method preserves most of the basic operational
features, as it only pertains to retrieving and setting of "values".

As much as object orientation _can_ ease programming of complex software,
the programmer has to be aware of the special cases and pitfalls of object
workings and properties (in every respect) to not create enormous
inefficiencies or righteously malfunctioning software.

(Using objects also requires a very detailed knowledge of the intentions
of their creator - deviation from the intended use, even unintended ones
because of a misconception caused by syntax equivalences, may produce
strange side effects and malfunctions that are hard to correct.)

The other area for changing of the behaviour of operators, directly
redefining them, can be even more distracting...
