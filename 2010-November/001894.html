Op 2010-11-15 21:24, Sieghard het geskryf:
> 
> VNC or RDP is _no_ "add-on" to an X, and a far way from being a substitute
> for its built-in networking abilities. VNC or RDP don't transmit graphic
> primitives to be drawn on the "other side"s display, but use a _local_

Yes I understand that. What the Wayland developers was trying to say is
that such functionality doesn't need to be inside the display API itself.
Such functionality could be built externally and only enabled when actually
needed. OS-X, Windows, BeOS, etc etc. doesn't have such "network
transparancy" built into the display API, yet they all found solutions to
work remotely over a network.


> screen to bluntly copy over the line - so there's no means to arrange the
> windows of individual applications on the display side, you only got the
> source screen and its arrangement. You might not even be able to adapt the
> screen size to your local display.

VirtualBox manages to do something like what you want. Normally it's a
single "desktop window", but you can enable "integration mode" where the
individual apps inside the VM appears as normal desktop apps on your
desktop. So there must be a way this same idea can be applied in Wayland.
Again without needing the client/server approach X11 currently has. I think
this is the point the Wayland developers was trying to make. X11 was
designed decades ago with much less technology and slower networks,
compared to today.


> No icons, no taskbar, no context menues, no "applets", nothing that may
> get into one's way at some inappropriate time. (But it has menues;)

JWM does the same, but comes default with a taskbar enabled. Menu setup is
also a simplified xml file so it's quick to add new menu items, and simply
right-click and select reload, to reload JWM without restarting X11.
Awesome! :)
