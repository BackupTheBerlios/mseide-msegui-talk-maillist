> Looks attractive but in Linux, SDL pulls a lot of heavy-weight dependecies.

True, but unfortunately that is the price you pay for x-platform (single
source in your app) sound support. We just went through this whole process
with our commercial apps that required sound. We tried multiple sound
libraries. Some were in one distro as standard, but not in others. Some can
be installed from the app repositories (eg: apt), some don't. It was a
rather big mess under Linux (as it always is for desktop apps and Linux).
SDL was simply the path with least resistance, as it supports multiple
sound libraries under Linux, so will pick what is available on the users
system, without us having to intervene in our applications.
