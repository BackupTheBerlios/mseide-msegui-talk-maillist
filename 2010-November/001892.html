> Op 2010-11-15 11:30, Martin Schreiber het geskryf:
> 
> that the "X11 networking display" will not be part of Wayland, but as an
> add-on like VNC, RDP etc..

VNC or RDP is _no_ "add-on" to an X, and a far way from being a substitute
for its built-in networking abilities. VNC or RDP don't transmit graphic
primitives to be drawn on the "other side"s display, but use a _local_
screen to bluntly copy over the line - so there's no means to arrange the
windows of individual applications on the display side, you only got the
source screen and its arrangement. You might not even be able to adapt the
screen size to your local display.

> add-on like VNC, RDP etc.. This should reduce/improve latency and gittery
> resizing of windows a lot as well.

This, I heard, was due to the dimishing use of X's asynchronous drawing
abilities - current applications are said to mainly use the synchronous
drawing facilities, which make drawing by itself a very costly and time
wasting process - much more so, if used over a network connection.
As Wayland probabely will _only_ implement the synchronous interface, you
will be able to appreciate its expectable merits...
