Not at all, Dvorak is actually easier to learn. I used Qwerty for 13+
years, and it took me a week after switching to Programmer Dvorak to type
at the same speed as I did in Qwerty (+- 65wpm). Now I have surpassed my
original Qwerty speed by far (averaging +-85wpm), and with a lot better
accuracy. No more tired fingers after long typing either. Dvorak was
designed for humans, Qwerty was designed to prevent typewriter jamming.


> BTW, I didn't yet know that "to Dvorak" means to change the keyboard. ;->

I'm quit fussy about what keyboards I use. I now use a Enermax Aurora
keyboard (very flat, very heavy and similar flat keys to a laptop). I
really love this keyboard. None of my keyboards have ever come as a Dvorak
layout though. All come standard as Qwerty, but I simply switch the layout
in my OS, and in the case of my Enermax, I also switched the key-caps
(makes things easier for keyboard shortcuts with one hand). But when you do
touch typing, you don't really need the key-caps changed at all.
