Op 2011-01-12 10:45, IvankoB het geskryf:
> 
> AFAIK, even embedded FireBird means some config (& access rights )
> efforts as long as SQLite3 doesn't :)

I can tell you straight that Embedded Firebird doesn't require config
files or special access rights. That's why we use it for some of our
projects. As far as I know you can even run your app and embedded
Firebird directly from a CD-ROM for "read-only" data.


> Also, SQLite has the ATTACH feature allowing explicitly to separate
> tables between different DB files - it adds much more 1) portability &
> 2) reliability.

That's more to do with an unreliable OS or filesystem, that can't
recover from critical events such as a power outage. In fact, we use JFS
filesystem for this exact reason, and we tested it with a Firebird
database and caused instant power outages to see what happens. Both the
filesystem (under Linux) and the database recovered every time without
problems.
