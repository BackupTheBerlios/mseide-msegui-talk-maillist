On Sun, 2011-01-16 at 04:16 +0700, Sri Wahono wrote:
> > But the RTF widget is required to implement DOC viewer (text styles &
> > colors, hyperlinks) etc ! Lack of RTF is like lack of head of winner of
> > beauty contest :)
> > And means deep knowledge of MSEgui just to be efficient.
> 
> BTW, HTML viewer should be considered than RTF. And it more universal
> than RTF (AFAIK RTF originally from MS)

Here I disagree. If you are going to implement a HTML widget component,
then everybody will think it must at least support HTML4, CSS1, CSS2,
CSS3 etc... It will take you years to implement - just look at KHTML,
Firefox etc... Why join that HUGE battle just for a handful of
developers using MSEgui. HTML people write such crap HTML source, you
will have to implement so many exceptions to the rule, it will be a
nightmare.

A RTF widget (at least a viewer) is slightly easier to implement, and
the viewing output is by far good enough for non-web browser
applications.

RTF might have been started by Microsoft (I think), but it is now a well
accepted standard cross multiple platforms: ie: Mac OS X uses it
extensively.
