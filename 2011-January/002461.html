> You probably never used MSEgui. ;-)

>From what I could see, MSEgui is just like Lazarus or Delphi in this
regard - please correct me if I am wrong. MSEgui (just like Delphi and
Lazarus) use TDataset descendants, TXXXDatabase & TXXXQuery components
and DB-aware components (duplicates of all non-db components).

That is a ridiculous way of designing maintainable database aware apps.
Yes it fits great with Delphi's "RAD development" sales pitch. But as
far as I'm concerned, that is just ok for small apps or prototypes.

I'm a big fan of true OOP design and the use of design patterns. There
must also be a clear separation between the persistence layer (long term
storage of data like in a database or file), the BOM (business objects
and rules) and lastly the GUI (desktop app, web interface etc). Using
DB-aware components and the whole TDataset things mixes all of that
together. Each gui component on a form is binded to a field in the
database - there you mix GUI and database structures. This also means
you can change your persistence of data from a RDBMS to a Tab or Comma
separated file - without a major overhaul of the application. As for the
db-aware components, that's a duplicate of non-db components and just
another place where bugs could creep in. Using something like MGM (which
is basically the Observer and Mediator design patterns working in sync),
I do not need to duplicate standard component and introduce new ones.
The MGM allows _any_ standard component to become "object-aware" and
data synchronization works in both directions.

Here is a screenshot of a DataModule under Delphi 7. This is an
"RAD-style" application I had to help develop and maintain. This was
also the reason I moved away from Delphi's db-aware style programming.
This app was anything but easy to maintain and very closely binded to a
specific type of database and database components.

  http://opensoft.homeip.net:8080/~graemeg/datamodule.png

Good OPF frameworks fixes all of the above and then some... Yes it's an
age old debate which I'll not get into here - there is enough
information available on the net already.
