Linux GUI is fast. Very fast. It may even run modern DirectX 9+ Win-32  
games via WINE+OpenGL layer on max quality :)
(proprietary NVidia drivers on GT240 DDR5, KDE3)

As to VESA kernel driver, it was a great (and graphic hardware  
independent!) solution until now but has becoming worse & worse as to  
support of newer hardware
(especially Intel embedded GPUs).
For instance it's impossible to have 2D accelerated FBDev+X on Intel G31+  
etc LGA+ chipsets (it can't even properly tune MTRR registers for  
write-combining
necessary for accelerating) thus we also have slow VirtualBox drawing etc.
Me wasted a week on attempts to achieve that 2D accelaration - with no  
success.
