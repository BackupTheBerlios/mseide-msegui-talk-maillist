On 29/03/2011 19:26, IvankoB wrote:
> During my own extensive testing of the A-G 'toolset,' I've found that
> well-optimized GDI+ drawing much faster overall than the same drawing being
> performed by A-G.

That could probably be due to the fact that GDI+ is only meant for one 
platform, so much can be optimized via assembler etc. AGG on the other 
hand runs on multiple platforms, so optimization is much harder to 
implement and will result in harder to maintained code. Those are 
options a developer must weigh up when designing such a library.

In AGG's defense, if you take a look at the AGG website, there is a 
section where they compare GDI+ output and AGG output of anti-aliasing. 
AGG quality being superior to GDI+'s output.
