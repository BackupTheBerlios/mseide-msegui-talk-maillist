Continuation from the author :

==============

The whole day has passed but me still can't get the MSE logic:


"tterminal.onprocfinished(const sender : tobject)" fires once the process  
finishes

It seems loigical that "sender" should be "tterminal" here.

But no! "(sender as tobject).classname"  shows that it's "tmseprocess".

procedure tmainfo.on_procfinished(const sender: TObject);
begin
   showmessage((sender as tobject).classname);
end;

that's not good if tterminal`-s are created in run-time and it's needed to  
trace which of them finished.

Currently, me use such workaround :

In unit "tmseprocess", a new property "termname : string" is introduced to  
which terminal name is written to when next terminal is created.

   t := tterminal.create(self);
   t.name := 'term' + inttostr(termNum);
   t.fprocess.termname := t.name;
   t.onprocfinished:= @on_procfinished;

to seen in that property which terminal finished its work.

However, the prperty "fprocess" has to move to the "public" section. And  
one more unit ("tmseprocess")  ahs to be used in the program.

Q:  optimal way ?
