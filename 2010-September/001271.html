BTW, the focus behaviour of the IDE is somewhat, err, unusual, as to say.
It does not really yield to another window made active by means of the
window manager. if the mouse cursor still sits within its reach, even if
the other window would normally get into possession of the mouse after
activation. I have to shift the mouse cursor into the window of interest to
heave that on top, although this should normally not be neccessary.

This effect occurs even among the IDE windows alone, and switching between
them redraws all affected windows several times, hiding and reshowing parts
of them in rapid succession, like the IDE was undecided what to really
prefer now....
It also occurred several times that I wasn't able to bring the main (menu)
window into the foreground, when it was covered partly by the component
palette - this would even stay in the foreground when it was _not_ active.

Most of these effects, BTW, did _not_ occur with the previous version(s),
where the editor also had the old behaviour of "jumping" to the nearest
word begin whether forward or backward. The new behaviour is really
awkward, always jumping to the _farthest_ end of the nearest word.
I _heartily_ _dislike_ it so. And it is _not_ configurable!
