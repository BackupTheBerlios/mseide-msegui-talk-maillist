Cope with the complexity, as I wrote - complexity is self-multiplying, add
some random documentation to a complex project, and reap something akin to
chaos.

The "casual user" of a programming system is indeed commonly a programmer;
but even a programmer need to know what his tools an building blocks do,
which amounts to the requirement of having some documentation about such
simple themes like
 - general structure of the system
 - interrelations of the system's components
 - purpose of each component
 - working of the components
 - how to use the components,
   what parameters they need, what ranges are acceptable,
   whether they throw exception, what exception they throw under which
   conditions...

There are quite some other points I didn't think of now, but does this
sound like something you prepare in a couple of spare minutes? I'd say no.

Preparing such documentation will also show the structural rigidity (or
lack thereof) of such a system. I'm afraid, in our case, there might show
up a couple places where structural rigidity had to give way to
"featuritis", when Martin reached high frequency version counting for
adding ever another option or special function to his MSEGUI everytime
someone asked (after my first such experience concerning the
"msechartrecorder", i refrained from further requests as yet), sometimes
breaking a lot of functional code in the process. Many such "break points"
might not even have shown up yet.
