It's turned out that by default, in WHERE clauses, ORACLE dectivates any  
indexes on fields participating in
"OR" or "IN (..)"  conditions. So, me had 16 secs query delay on joining  
250000recs table1 against 400000recs table1 - not large tables, sure.
The problem was solved ( new delay is 1ms ) by using UNION instead of OR  
but this solution isn't always accetable
because of some postprocessing by the UNION.
Is there ORACLE tuning option(s) to make its optimizer smarter ?
