> >BTW: Interesting historical circuit; what it's good for? It seems to be
...
> Looks like a UHF-2-VHF converter although variable capasitors appear a
> bit big for such a high frequency circuit :) 

Could well be; the variable capacitors' size isn't really exaggerated for
that time - the size only gives rise to geometric resonances, which might
have not had any impact if well separated from the working frequencies.
And beyond that, they could even be some kind of "integrated circuit"
elements constituting complete resonators; they were called "butterfly
capacitors" and included an inductive element connecting two "wings" each
of the metallized areas consituting the plates.
And after all, the capacity is very much influenced by the disk material's
dielectric constant.
