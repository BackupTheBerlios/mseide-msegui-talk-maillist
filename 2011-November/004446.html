Some results of tests of POSTGRESQL (9.0) performance penalty on  
device_mapper-encrypted "loop" file-device  (LINUX) :

Master table: 100.000 recs
Details table: 100.000*10(master_refs) = 1.000.000 recs

--------------------------
Filling with data from dump file:

Not encrypted storage: 11 secs
AES-encrypted storage: 16 secs
--------------------------
Non indexed two tables SELECT join:

Not encrypted storage: 1.6 secs
AES-encrypted storage: 2.4 secs

-------------------------
Indexed two tables SELECT join:

Not encrypted storage: 0.3 secs
AES-encrypted storage: 0.3 secs


So, the penalty looks quite acceptable (sure because the kernel VFS -layer  
& encryptor are used here).
Though, my machine may be not a good testacase anymore - now  it has 4G  
DDR3-1333 thus huge FS caches...
