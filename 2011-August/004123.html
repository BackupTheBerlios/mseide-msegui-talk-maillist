Do you execute pacman directly (via fork/exec) or through a shell?
If the latter, you might try to add a "2>&1" to the command line, merging
the stdout and stderr streams in one, so no race condition between the
streams may arise for the terminal component.

(With unusual output conditions, it might be advisable to check what's
output on what stream - on Linux, amny programs still use stdout and stderr
for their intended purposes or at least to distinguish "important" from
"normal" output.)
