> I have the same experience. For example making polished in grid editing has a
> big user benefit but is not trivial to implement and needs most the time
> special handling of the events. MSEide+MSEgui is especially made to increase

I have no doubts that you have made this very easy for an experience
MSEgui coder. You do have some interesting and unique ideas in MSEgui.

I would just like to add how I accomplish the same thing with MGM. For
specialized cases I create a custom mediator (a descendant of one of
the generic mediators). This custom mediator contains all the event
handler (of things like validation, lookup lists etc) or even custom
paint events (as in the case of some of our StringGrid based grids).
That custom mediator can then be registered and reused wherever inline
grid editing is required.

The nice thing is that again, you can reuse that mediator all over the
place. Make changes to it, and you know all areas in your program that
used it are automatically correct on the next recompile. This I find
much harder to accomplish with RAD style development - no matter how
many data modules or TFrame's you try and use. But that's just my
opinion. ;-)

In the ending, each development team needs to evaluate and decide what
style of coding works best for them.
