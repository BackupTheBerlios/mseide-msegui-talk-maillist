_Probabely_ they were not complete then, I guess.
But even if they are now, it's not neccessary in general to switch to
another locale completely, as the localisation can be set rather finely
grained. There are quite a number of environment variables resonsible for
different aspects of localisation, their settings are listed by the simple
command "locale". The names are rather self-explanatory, and most are
independent from each other, with the exception of "LANG", which defines a
preset value, and "LC_ALL", which is an override taking precedence over all
the other settings.
BTW, there's even a man page for locale and the pertaining environment
variables.
